    Understand NAT Behavior

    Why NAT Affects Experience

    Strong bandwidth does not guarantee good peer connectivity. NAT determines whether external traffic can reach your device. Strict mappings often force relays, increasing latency.

    Common Causes

    • Double NAT (modem + router both doing NAT)
    • UPnP disabled or unsupported
    • Carrier-grade NAT (CGNAT)
    • Overly strict firewall rules

    Why Run This Test

    Knowing your NAT type explains unstable connections and helps decide whether to enable UPnP, set port forwarding, or request a public IP from your ISP.

    FAQ

    Do you collect my data?

    No. The test runs in your browser and only contacts public STUN servers.

    Why does it say “Possibly symmetric NAT”?

    If different STUN servers return different mapped ports, symmetric NAT is likely.

    How can I improve my NAT type?

    Try UPnP, port forwarding, or requesting a public IP. Multiple routers usually worsen NAT.
