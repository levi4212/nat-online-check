How to Improve Your NAT Type

Try these actions in order. Results depend on your network and ISP.

Quick Checklist

  • Avoid double NAT (modem + router both doing NAT).
  • Enable UPnP on your router.
  • Use port forwarding or DMZ (with caution).
  • Bridge your modem and let the router dial directly.
  • Request a public IPv4 or enable IPv6 from your ISP.
  • Avoid carrier-grade NAT (CGNAT).

Suggested Steps

1. Check for Double NAT

If both modem and router are doing NAT, good NAT types are hard to achieve. Bridge the modem if possible.

2. Enable UPnP

UPnP allows devices to request port mappings automatically. Re-test after enabling.

3. Manual Port Forwarding

Forward ports for specific games/apps and keep a fixed local IP for the device.

4. Use DMZ (Caution)

DMZ maps all ports to one device. It can improve connectivity but increases risk.

5. Request Public IP / IPv6

Under CGNAT, you may need to ask your ISP for a public IPv4. IPv6 can also help.

6. Device & Network Tweaks

Updating firmware, easing strict firewall rules, or tuning QoS can improve results.

Notes

  • ISP policies vary widely. Always rely on real test results.
  • Symmetric NAT is the hardest to improve without ISP support.
